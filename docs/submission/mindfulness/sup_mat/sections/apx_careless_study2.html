To maintain consistency and comparability with Study 1, we applied the same procedures for identifying and excluding participants with inattentive or careless responding in Study 2.

Phase 1: Pre-EMA Data Quality Assessment

Threshold for Identifying Careless Responders

As in Study 1, we calculated four indices—Longstring Index, IRV, Even-Odd Inconsistency Index, and Mahalanobis Distance—using responses from the administered questionnaires (DERS, SCS, and DASS-21). Participants with scores exceeding the 95th percentile on more than two indices were flagged as careless responders.

A total of 4 participants met these criteria and were excluded from further EMA data collection.

Phase 2: Ensuring Response Quality in EMA Data

For the EMA phase, we evaluated response quality at the individual level using repeated administrations of the 8-item State Self-Compassion Scale. The same indices and thresholds from Study 1 were used to ensure consistent criteria.

Integration of Metrics to Determine Careless Responding

To identify participants flagged on multiple indices, we used the following R script to calculate the overlap of flagged participants across different combinations of indices:

vectors <- list(mahad_bad, longstring_bad, irv_bad, even_odd_bad)

# Function to find shared elements across combinations
shared_counts <- map(2:4, ~ {
  combos <- combn(vectors, .x, simplify = FALSE)
  shared <- map(combos, ~ Reduce(intersect, .x)) %>% unlist() %>% unique()
  length(shared)
}) %>% set_names(paste0("shared_by_", 2:4))
